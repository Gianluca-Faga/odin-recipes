Lasagna recipe



Classic oven-baked lasagna

Lasagna, a classic Italian dish, is a culinary delight loved by people worldwide. Its layers of tender pasta, flavorful tomato sauce, savory meat, and creamy cheese create a symphony of taste. Originating in ancient Rome, lasagna has evolved into a versatile dish with regional variations. Whether it's a family gathering or a festive occasion, lasagna brings people together, creating lasting memories around the dining table.

Ingredients

  • 350 g Durum wheat semolina
  • 150 g All-purpose flour (Type 00)
  • 250 g Spinach
  • 2 Eggs
  • 3 Egg yolks
  • 300 g Beef (coarsely ground mixed beef)
  • 150 g Pancetta
  • 50 g Carrots
  • 50 g Celery
  • 50 g Golden onions
  • 100 g Red wine
  • 300 g Tomato passata
  • Vegetable broth, as needed
  • 1 tablespoon Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 70 g Butter
  • 70 g All-purpose flour (Type 00)
  • 1 liter Whole milk
  • Salt, to taste
  • Nutmeg, to taste
  • Butter, as needed
  • 270 g Parmigiano Reggiano cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetable broth and keep it warm.
  2. Take the pancetta, cut it into strips, and finely chop it.
  3. Prepare a fine mixture with carrots, onions, and celery, and set it aside.
  4. In a saucepan, pour a drizzle of oil and add the pancetta. Sauté it well for a few minutes.
  5. Then add the vegetable mixture and sauté for 5-6 minutes to enhance the flavors.
  6. Add the ground meat, mix well, and increase the heat. Let the meat brown slowly, sealing the juices and becoming tender.
  7. Deglaze with red wine and wait for it to completely evaporate.
  8. Add the tomato puree and stir. The ragù should cook for two hours. Once it starts boiling again, add a little hot broth, one or two ladles. Then let the ragù cook for at least two hours.
  9. You should cook it with the lid on, but not completely closed. Check and stir occasionally, adding more broth if needed, and let it cook for the indicated time.
  10. Place the spinach in a pan, add a little water, cover with a lid, and let it cook until wilted, approximately 5-6 minutes in total.
  11. At this point, drain the spinach, let it cool down, and squeeze out the excess water.
  12. Transfer the spinach to a blender and puree until you obtain 100 g of spinach puree.
  13. Now, on a work surface, place the semolina and all-purpose flour, add the spinach puree, and create a well in the center.
  14. Add the lightly beaten eggs and egg yolks to the well.
  15. Start kneading from the center, so that the eggs don't spill out of the well. Knead well until you obtain a homogeneous dough.
  16. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes at room temperature.
  17. In a small saucepan, heat the milk without boiling it.
  18. In another pan, melt the butter. Add the flour all at once and cook for a few minutes until you get a golden roux.
  19. Gradually pour the warm milk into the pan, stirring well.
  20. Add salt and flavor with nutmeg.
  21. Continue stirring until you obtain a creamy béchamel sauce.
  22. Transfer the sauce to a bowl, cover it with plastic wrap placed directly on the surface, and set it aside.
  23. After the 30 minutes have passed, take the dough and cut off a piece. Cover the remaining dough with plastic wrap to prevent it from drying out.
  24. Using a little semolina to prevent sticking, flatten the dough slightly on the work surface to form a rectangle and roll out the pasta using a pasta machine.
    Start with a thicker setting, then fold the dough onto itself and pass it through again. When it becomes too moist, dust it with some semolina.
    Gradually reduce the thickness until you obtain a sheet slightly over half a millimeter thick. Transfer it to a work surface and cut it into 30x20 cm (12x8 inch) rectangles.
    Continue in this way until you finish the dough, and in the meantime, bring a pot of salted water to a boil.
    When it starts boiling, cook one sheet at a time, wait for 30-40 seconds, and using a slotted spoon, drain it.
    Transfer it to a tray with a kitchen towel and pat it dry to remove excess water.
    Continue in this way, placing the cooked sheets side by side without overlapping them.
  25. Once the ragù is ready, adjust the salt and pepper seasoning and proceed with assembling the lasagna.
  26. Take a large 30x20 cm (12x8 inch) lasagna dish and butter the bottom. Add a thin layer of béchamel sauce and a layer of ragù.
    Then place the first sheet of pasta, add a layer of béchamel sauce and ragù. Add grated Parmesan cheese and place another sheet of pasta on top.
    Repeat this process, alternating layers of béchamel sauce, ragù, and grated cheese, until you have five layers.
    After placing the last sheet, cover it completely with ragù.
    Sprinkle with grated cheese, add small pieces of butter, and bake in a preheated static oven at 170°C (340°F) for 40 minutes.
  27. The Bolognese lasagna is ready to be served.
